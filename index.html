TartanHacks

TartanHacks is an annual, student-run hackathon run by Scottylabs.

February 6-7, 2015

Registration

Registration opens on Monday January 19th at 6pm. We offer priority registration to freshmen and female hackers at 5pm.

FAQ

Q: How many people are you accepting initially?

A: 350

Q: What's the registration process like?

A: We give priority registration to first-years and female hackers. They'll be able to register an hour early at 5pm. After registration opens, about 350 spots will be given away first come, first serve. If you don't get a spot, you can still get on the waitlist, and there's a good chance you'll still be able to participate. More specific waitlist instructions will be sent out before the hackathon.

Q: How much does it cost to participate in TartanHacks?

A: Nothing. TartanHacks is free for all current CMU students.

Q: Can graduate students participate?

A: Graduate students are welcome at TartanHacks!

Q: I'm not a CS major, can I still join?

A: Yes! All majors and programs, even if they aren't technical, can participate in TartanHacks. Before you come, it's probably a good idea to brush up on your programming skills: check out the web dev weeks website to find some awesome tutorials on web development.

Q: I want to do a hardware hack. Is this okay?

A: Yes! Hardware hacks are welcome, but we don't provide equipment. If you need special accommodations for your demo, let an organizer know and we'll do our best to help you out.

Q: Who runs TartanHacks?

A: TartanHacks is run by ScottyLabs, a student organization at Carnegie Mellon that develops apps for CMU and runs educational events, like TartanHacks. ScottyLabs isn't affiliated with any academic department, and all of our funding comes from our sponsor companies.

Q: What kind of prizes can I win?

A: There will be a grand prize for our hackathon, which will be awarded by a panel of judges from Carnegie Mellon. Our sponsors will also award prizes in a variety of categories, for creativity, technical prowess, and novelty.

Q: How can I get in touch with the organizers?

A: Send an email to info@scottylabs.org!
